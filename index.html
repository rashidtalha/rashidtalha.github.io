Rashid Talha

I am enrolled in the mathematics section of the Postgraduate Diploma Programme at the Abdus Salam International Centre for Theoretical Physics (ICTP), Italy.

Studies:

I hold a bachelor's degree in mathematics from the National University of Sciences and Technology (NUST), Pakistan. I was awarded the President's Gold Medal as the highest-ranking student in my cohort, and the Rector's Gold Medal for the best final-year project. My thesis titled “Ehresmann Connections on Fibre Bundles” was supervised by Dr Tooba Feroze.

Previously, I received a DipHE in mathematics and physics from the University of Warwick, UK. This qualification comprises the first two years of the joint integrated master's programme in mathematics and physics.

Research:

My research interests lie at the interface of mathematics and theoretical physics, with a particular focus on differential geometry and its applications to gravitational physics, including black holes and aspects of quantum gravity.

I am also interested in gauge and topological field theories, and in the algebraic, geometric, and topological ideas that underpin them.
